Posture and Roll Step

While the basic movement and posture is displayed in the video above, it is important to note that bands may have different versions of the same movement. The Marching Virginians for example, when come to attention, "pop" thier heads and bring thier knee up before locking into attention.

Drill

Drill is the basic form of any given marching band show, consisting of pictures of the field with dots or symbols representing individual members of the band. Each individual picture is called a set and the movement of a field perforamance is simply the band moving between pictures, from set to set.

Commands and Terms

Attention
The band comes to attention, unmoving with proper posture, ready for further commands
At Ease
The band can relax
Forward March
Band marches forward
Backwards March
Band marches on thier toes backwards while remaining facing forwards
To the Rear
Band spins around to move in the opposite direction
Quater / Half Turn to the Left / Right
The band turns either 45˚ or 90˚to the given direction
Band Halt
Band stops marching
Slide march
Band marches with thier bodies straight but with thier hips and legs facing 90˚ in another direction
Horn pop
Instruments are raised to a much greater degree than normal, they are "popped" up
Horn Flash
A movement of the instruments, such as swaying them side to side
